Riding Holy

March 2023 - January 2024

Skate-riding, vampire-slaying priest game? You bet! Riding Holy is a third-person, action game prototype and was the longest video game project I have partaken in, having suffered a game engine change (from Unity (C#) to Unreal) midway through development. It taught me valuable long-term programming practices, such as maintaining auxiliary documentation, comprehensible to the whole team. I was mainly responsible for implementing the enemy AI (having implemented, for instance, behaviour trees in both Unity (C#) and Unreal), and the player's abilities and core movement (e.g.: rail grinding).

Cosmic Cat-astrophy

May 2023 - June 2024

Cosmic Cat-astrophy is a physics-based 3D platformer and stands as one of the most mechanically complex games I have had the pleasure of developing: from planetary gravitational pull and ragdoll behaviour, to local multiplayer and puzzle solving systems. This Unity project (C#) also accounted for detailed game design documentation (e.g.: post mortem and playtesting reports) and recurring devlogs. Through this game, I learnt the importance of managing a development scope and ensuring a polished vertical slice. I was responsible for implementing the ragdoll behaviour, its interactions with gravitational forces and local multiplayer.

Multi-Agent Ant Colony

May 2023 - June 2023

Science has a tendency of mimicking behaviours it observes in nature to try to solve complex problems... I decided to have a go at this approach by developing an ant colony environment in Python where diverse types of agents (e.g.: reactive and deliberative agents) were studied and analysed regarding their efficiency in exploring a map for interest points and keeping their colony's food storage high. Communication through evaporating pheromones and cooperation to carry larger food piles were some of the implemented multi-agent behaviours. In a larger scope, the results could be easily recontextualised in pathfinding problems or even for enemy swarm behaviours in video games.

Pathfinding Algorithms

22nd September 2022 - 2nd October 2022

Efficient pathfinding algorithms allowing characters to traverse the surrounding environments easily and quickly are essential for any video game that intends to simulate believable and autonomous movement. This project, developed in Unity (C#), allowed me to explore a range of algorithms with increasing levels of complexity, leading me to analyse trade-offs between efficiency and computational resources.

Decision Making Algorithms

2nd October 2022 - 23rd October 2022

The methods through which characters decide their next best course of action can make or break games, with the potential of affecting, for instance, immersion and overall difficulty. Through this project, I implemented a multitude of different algorithms in Unity (C#) which allowed me to explore the topic of decision making - greedier perspectives (e.g.: Goal Oriented Behaviour) to simulating future world states (e.g: Monte Carlo Tree Search), behaviour trees and enemy formations.

Cel Shading

1st January 2023 - 15th January 2023

Written in C++ and exploiting the OpenGL API, this program attempts to reproduce two different materials (in this case, cork and stainless steel) considering a specific shading style - cel shading. As a solo project, I had to follow a set of development milestones to accomplish my original goals. More importantly though, this experience gave me insights into how procedural materials and shading works inside video game engines.

Inverse Kinematics

April 2024

Performed as a small side project due to my growing curiosity on the topic, this Unreal prototype deepened my knowledge on inverse kinematics (or procedural animations), where all the animations follow a given procedure - a set of logic rules grounded on diverse, easily editable parameters. In a wider scope, this project made me understand how distinct areas of game development (animation and programming) can be fused together to convey fascinating and believable results.
