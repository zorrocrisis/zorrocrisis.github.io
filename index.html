Game Development

Over the last years, I have been fortunate enough to participate and hone my game development skills in multiple solo and group projects. The following games are examples which highlight the most important takeaways from this journey.

Riding Holy

March, 2023 - January, 2024

Skate-riding, vampire-slaying priest game? You bet! Riding Holy is a third-person, action game prototype and was the longest video game project I have partaken in, having suffered a game engine change (from Unity to Unreal) midway through development. It taught me valuable long-term programming practices, such as maintaining auxiliary documentation, comprehensible to the whole team. I was mainly responsible for implementing the enemy AI (having implemented, for instance, behaviour trees in both Unity and Unreal), and the player's abilities and core movement.

Cosmic Cat-astrophy

May, 2023 - June, 2024

Cosmic Cat-astrophy is a physics-based 3D platformer and stands as one of the most mechanically complex games I have had the pleasure of developing: from planetary gravitational pull and ragdoll behaviour, to local multiplayer and puzzle solving systems. The project also accounted for detailed game design documentation (e.g.: post mortem and playtesting reports) and recurring devlogs. Through this game, I learnt the importance of managing a development scope and ensuring a polished vertical slice. I was responsible for implementing the ragdoll behaviour, its interactions with gravitational forces and local multiplayer.

Rootie & Twootie Operation Chaos and the Rogue Numbers 3

3rd February, 2023 - 5th February, 2023

What a long name, huh? Rootie & Twootie is a top-down 2D shooter which resulted from my first ever game jam (Global Game Jam 2023), having only lasted 3 days! As such, this project showed how, despite the tight deadlines, working in a team environment can drastically boost the game development process, in both creativity and critical thinking. I was responsible for the main player-environment interactions, namely the system which allows the main characters to perform mathematical operations on the numbers (enemies).

Sleepless

May, 2024 - July, 2024

Who needs sleep anyways? Sleepless is a horror visual novel developed during the 2024 edition of the Josei Jam. This project taught me how to maintain a cohesive storyline and set of characters, underlied by narrative sequences and programmed dialogue states, seeing as though I played both the role of a programmer and writer in this game's development.

Tech Projects

Besides game development projects, I have gained important insights from implementing tech prototypes, which span across multiple underlying systems of video games.

Pathfinding Algorithms

22nd September 2022 - 2nd October, 2022

Efficient pathfinding algorithms allowing characters to traverse the surrounding environments easily and quickly are essential for any video game that intends to simulate believable and autonomous movement. This project allowed me to explore a range of algorithms with increasing levels of complexity, leading me to analyse trade-offs between efficiency and computational resources.

Decision Making Algorithms

2nd October, 2022 - 23rd October, 2022

Ever wondered how video game characters decided their next best course of action? Through this project, I learnt a multitude of different algorithms which allow me to answer this question. From greedier perspectives (e.g.: Goal Oriented Behaviour) to simulating future world states (e.g: Monte Carlo Tree Search), I also implemented behaviour trees and enemy formations.

Cel Shading

1st January, 2023 - 15th January, 2023

A shaders projects which attempts to reproduce two different materials (in this case, cork and stainless steel), adapted to a shading style. As a solo project, I had to follow a set of development milestones to accomplish my original goals. More importantly though, this experience gave me insights into how procedural materials and shading works inside video game engines.
